About Duette Immigration

Duette Immigration brings together the experience and client dedication of Svetlana Demb and Masha Gubelman, two licensed Canadian immigration professionals. Our partnership was created to offer individuals, families, and employers knowledgeable guidance and a stronger, more comprehensive immigration practice.

We support clients through every stage of the Canadian immigration process with care, clarity, and strategic advice. Each case receives thoughtful attention and a personalized approach focused on achieving our clients' goals.

Our practice covers a wide range of immigration matters, including permanent and temporary residence, family sponsorship, study and work permits, provincial nominee programs, business immigration, and citizenship.

At Duette Immigration, partnership is at the heart of what we do — working together with our clients to make the immigration journey clear, smooth, and successful.

Our Services

We offer comprehensive support across a wide range of Canadian immigration matters.

Permanent Residence

Expert guidance through Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and other permanent residence pathways to help you call Canada home.

Temporary Residence

Visitor visas, temporary resident permits, and extensions to support your short-term stay in Canada.

Family Sponsorship

Reunite with your loved ones through spousal, partner, parent, and dependent child sponsorship applications.

Study Permits

Navigate the study permit process and open the door to world-class Canadian education.

Work Permits

Employer-specific and open work permits, LMIA applications, and strategies to build your career in Canada.

Provincial Nominee Programs

Province-specific immigration streams tailored to regional labour market needs and community growth.

Business Immigration

Pathways for entrepreneurs, investors, and self-employed individuals looking to contribute to Canada's economy.

Citizenship

Guidance through the Canadian citizenship application process, from eligibility assessment to the citizenship ceremony.
